Precautions & Health Plan

Major precautions, healthy foods, and benefits every patient should know.

01

Monitor Blood Sugar

Check fasting glucose and HbA1c as advised. Regular monitoring helps detect changes early and prevents silent progression.

02

Reduce Added Sugar

Limit sweets, sugary drinks, packaged juices, desserts, and refined snacks because they can rapidly increase blood glucose.

03

Choose High-Fiber Meals

Fiber slows sugar absorption and improves fullness. Add vegetables, lentils, beans, oats, seeds, and whole grains.

04

Walk After Meals

A 10-15 minute walk after meals can help reduce post-meal glucose spikes and improve insulin sensitivity.

05

Maintain Healthy Weight

Losing even 5-10% body weight can significantly improve insulin resistance, blood pressure, and metabolic health.

06

Sleep 7-9 Hours

Poor sleep can increase hunger hormones, stress hormones, and blood sugar. Keep a regular sleep schedule.

07

Quit Smoking

Smoking worsens blood vessel damage, insulin resistance, and heart risk. Quitting improves long-term outcomes.

08

Follow Medical Advice

Do not ignore symptoms or abnormal reports. Consult a doctor for medication, diet planning, and follow-up testing.
